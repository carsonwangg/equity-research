# Micron Q1 FY2026 Earnings Call Transcript
**Date:** December 17, 2025

## Key Highlights

- **Revenue:** $13.6 billion (record), up 57% YoY, 21% QoQ
- **DRAM Revenue:** $10.8 billion (record), up 69% YoY (79% of total)
- **NAND Revenue:** $2.7 billion (record), up 22% YoY
- **Gross Margin:** 56.8% (+11 pp QoQ)
- **EPS:** $4.78 (record), +167% YoY
- **Free Cash Flow:** $3.9 billion (quarterly record, 20%+ above prior record)

## Records Achieved
- Total company revenue
- DRAM and NAND revenue
- HBM revenue
- Data center revenue
- Revenue in each business unit

## Strategic Highlights

### HBM Leadership
- Completed price and volume agreements for **entire CY2026 HBM supply** including HBM4
- HBM TAM CAGR: ~40% through CY2028
- HBM TAM: $35B (2025) → $100B (2028) - 2 years ahead of prior outlook
- 2028 HBM TAM larger than entire DRAM market in CY2024
- HBM4 on track to ramp with high yields in CQ2 2026, >11 Gbps speed
- HBM4E customization opportunities with customers

### Technology Progress
- 1-gamma: Primary driver of DRAM bit growth in CY2026
- 1-gamma to be majority of DRAM output in H2 CY2026
- Development underway for 1-delta and 1-epsilon nodes
- G9 NAND: Record QLC mix, becoming largest node later in FY2026
- CY2025: Record year for quality (internal + customer measures)

### Data Center Strength
- Server units CY2025: Now high-teens % growth (vs 10% prior)
- Data center NAND exceeded $1B in Q1
- First PCIe Gen6 SSD (world's first)
- 122TB and 245TB G9 SSDs entering hyperscale qualification

### Market Outlook - Supply Tightness
- Industry supply "substantially short of demand for foreseeable future"
- HBM 3-to-1 trade ratio with DDR5 (increasing with future generations)
- Cleanroom lead times lengthening globally
- Tightness expected through and beyond CY2026
- CY2026 industry bit shipments: ~20% growth (constrained by supply)
- Micron bit shipments: ~20% in CY2026

### CapEx Increase
- FY2026 CapEx: ~$20B (up from $18B prior)
- Primarily for HBM and 1-gamma supply
- Idaho fab 1 first wafers: Mid-CY2027 (pulled in from H2 CY2027)
- Idaho fab 2: Construction 2026, operational by end of 2028
- New York fab: Groundbreaking early CY2026, supply in 2030+
- Singapore HBM packaging: Contributing meaningfully CY2027
