# Micron Q4 FY2025 Earnings Call Transcript
**Date:** September 23, 2025

## Key Highlights

- **Revenue:** $11.3 billion (record), up 46% YoY, 22% QoQ
- **DRAM Revenue:** $9.0 billion (record), up 69% YoY
- **Gross Margin:** 45.7%
- **EPS:** $3.03 (record)
- **Full Year Revenue:** $37.4 billion (record), up 49% YoY
- **Full Year Gross Margin:** 41% (+17 pp YoY)

## Full Year Highlights

### Data Center Dominance
- Data center: 56% of total revenue (record)
- Data center gross margins: 52%
- HBM + High-capacity DIMMs + LP server DRAM: $10 billion (5x YoY)
- Data center SSD: Record revenue and market share
- Sole supplier of LP DRAM in data center

### HBM Business
- Q4 HBM revenue: ~$2 billion (~$8B annualized run rate)
- HBM share on track to match overall DRAM share by CQ3
- HBM customer base expanded to 6 customers
- HBM4 samples shipped: 2.8 TB/s bandwidth, 11+ Gbps pin speeds
- HBM4E: Standard products + customization option (higher gross margins)
- Pricing agreements for majority of HBM3E CY2026 supply

### Technology Milestones
- 1γ DRAM reached mature yields in record time (50% faster than prior gen)
- First in industry to ship 1γ DRAM
- First 1γ server DRAM revenue from major hyperscale customer
- G9 NAND TLC and QLC ramping, qualified for enterprise storage
- Mobile managed NAND development ceased (focus on higher ROI)

### AI Integration
- 30-40% productivity uplift in select GenAI use cases (code generation)
- 5x increase in wafer images analyzed
- 2x useful data/telemetry collected from fab tools
- AI accelerating design simulation and verification

## Market Outlook
- Server units CY2025: ~10% growth (up from mid-single digits)
- Traditional servers strengthening (AI agents driving workloads)
- PC units CY2025: Mid-single-digit % growth
- Smartphones CY2025: Low-single-digit % growth
- DRAM demand CY2025: High-teens % growth
- NAND demand CY2025: Low-to-mid-teens % growth
- CY2026: Further DRAM supply tightness expected

## Q1 FY2026 Guidance
- **Revenue:** $12.5B ± $300M (record)
- **Gross Margin:** 51.5% ± 100 bps
- **EPS:** $3.75 ± $0.15 (record)
- **CapEx:** ~$4.5B (baseline for FY2026)
