## 10-K FY2025 Key Highlights

### Business Overview
- **Industry Position:** Global leader in innovative memory and storage solutions
- **Products:** DRAM, NAND, NOR memory and storage products
- **Brands:** Micron®, Crucial®

### Business Unit Reorganization (FY2025 Q4)
| Business Unit | Focus |
|--------------|-------|
| **CMBU (Cloud Memory)** | Hyperscale cloud, HBM for all data center |
| **CDBU (Core Data Center)** | Mid-tier cloud, enterprise, OEM data center; DC SSDs |
| **MCBU (Mobile & Client)** | Mobile, client PCs, Crucial consumer products |
| **AEBU (Automotive & Embedded)** | Automotive, industrial, consumer embedded |

### Technology Leadership
- **DRAM:** First 1γ (1-gamma) production node with EUV lithography
- **NAND:** G9 node ramping (industry's 9th-generation 3D NAND)
- **HBM:** HBM3E 12-high in volume; HBM4 sampling to customers

### FY2025 Revenue by Technology
| Technology | Revenue | YoY Growth |
|------------|---------|------------|
| DRAM | $28.58B | +62% |
| NAND | $8.50B | +18% |
| Other (NOR) | ~$0.35B | +8% |
| **Total** | ~$37.4B | +48% |

### Manufacturing Footprint
- **Fabs:** Taiwan, Singapore, Japan, US (Idaho, Virginia), China
- **Expansion:** Idaho Fab 1 (mid-2027), Idaho Fab 2 (2028), New York (2030+)
- **HBM Packaging:** Singapore facility contributing 2027+

### Key Risk Factors
1. **ASP Volatility** - DRAM/NAND pricing highly cyclical
2. **Geopolitical** - Taiwan concentration, China CAC restrictions
3. **Competition** - Samsung, SK hynix, CXMT, YMTC
4. **Technology Execution** - Node transitions, HBM yield
5. **CapEx Intensity** - $18-20B annual investment required

### Key Strategic Updates

#### HBM Leadership
- **CY2026 HBM supply** completely committed (price + volume)
- **HBM4** on track for volume production CQ2 2026
- **HBM TAM:** ~$35B in 2025 → $100B by 2028 (40% CAGR)
- HBM4E customization engagements underway

#### Technology Node Progress
- **1-gamma DRAM:** Primary bit growth driver CY2026; majority output H2 2026
- **G9 NAND:** Ramping with strong yields; majority node by late FY2026
- **Beyond 1-gamma:** 1-delta and 1-epsilon in development

#### Manufacturing Capacity Expansion
- **FY26 CapEx:** ~$20B (up from prior $18B estimate)
- **Idaho Fab 1:** First wafer mid-CY2027 (accelerated)
- **Idaho Fab 2:** Construction 2026, operational end of 2028
- **New York Fab:** Groundbreaking early CY2026, supply 2030+
- **Japan (Hiroshima):** Adding cleanroom for advanced nodes
- **Singapore:** HBM advanced packaging contributing CY2027
- **India:** Assembly/test pilot production started, ramp 2026

#### Market Outlook
- **CY2025 DRAM demand:** Low 20% growth (raised from high teens)
- **CY2025 NAND demand:** High teens % growth (raised from low-mid teens)
- **CY2026 industry supply:** +~20% for both DRAM and NAND
- **Supply/Demand Balance:** Tight conditions expected to persist beyond CY2026

### Management Commentary Highlights

> "Micron is in the best competitive position in its history and is one of the semiconductor industry's biggest enablers of AI." - Sanjay Mehrotra, CEO

- Record revenue, gross margin, and EPS expected for Q2 and full FY2026
- Business performance expected to strengthen through the year
- Memory has shifted from "system component to strategic asset" for AI
- Despite significant efforts, unable to meet all customer demand across segments
